Biographical Background

Louise Bourgeois was born in Choisy-le-Roi, a suburb of Paris, France, and passed away in New York, USA. She held dual French and American nationality (later naturalized as an American). She grew up in a family that ran a tapestry restoration and sales business. Although her childhood was immersed in an environment of art and craftsmanship, she suffered psychological trauma due to her father's long-term extramarital affair and her mother's chronic illness. These complex family relationships and emotional conflicts became the source of her artistic creation throughout her life.

In her early years, she studied mathematics at the Sorbonne in Paris, then turned to the field of art. In the 1930s, she entered the École des Beaux-Arts in Paris to study painting and sculpture. In 1938, she married Robert Goldwater, an American art historian, moved to New York with him and settled there, starting her artistic career of more than 70 years.
